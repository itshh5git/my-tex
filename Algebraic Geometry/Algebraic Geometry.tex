\begin{definition}
    Let $k$ be a fixed algebraically closed field. We define affine $n$-space over $k$, denoted $\mathbf{A}_k^n$ or simply $\mathbf{A}^n$, to be the set of all $n$-tuples of elements of $k$. An element $P \in \mathbf{A}^n$ will be called a point, and if $P=\left(a_1, \ldots, a_n\right)$ with $a_i \in k$, then the $a_i$ will be called the coordinates of $P$.
\end{definition}
